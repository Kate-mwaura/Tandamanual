Page 3: Agent Onboarding - Getting Started

A. Account Creation and Verification

  1. Sign-up Process: To become a Tanda agent, start by downloading the TandaPay App from your device's app store or dial *712# from your phone. Once you have access to the app, fill out the registration form, providing accurate and up-to-date information about your business.
  2. Email and Phone Verification: As part of the registration process, you will be required to verify your email address and phone number. A verification code will be sent to both your email and phone. Enter these codes in the app to complete the verification process.
  3. Required Documentation (KYC): During registration, you will need to provide Know Your Customer (KYC) documentation, which may include your national ID, business registration documents, and proof of address. Make sure to submit clear and legible copies to expedite the approval process.

Visual Chart: Agent Sign-up Process

  1. Download TandaPay App / Dial *712#.
  2. Fill out the registration form.
  3. Verify email and phone number using codes received.
  4. Submit required KYC documents (National ID, Business Registration, Proof of Address).
  5. Await account approval.

Page 4: Agent Onboarding - Training and Operations

B. Product Training

  • Overview of Tanda's features: As a Tanda agent, you'll have access to a wide range of features that enable you to offer airtime, utility bill payments, deposits, and withdrawals all in one place.
  • Agent-specific functionalities: Tanda's platform includes functionalities tailored specifically for agents, such as a unified float account, a single statement for all transactions, and access to float overdrafts.
  • Best practices: To excel as a Tanda agent, adhere to best practices such as maintaining a sufficient float balance, providing exceptional customer service, and staying informed about updates and changes to the platform.

C. Integration and Support

  • Tools and resources: Tanda provides various tools and resources to assist you in managing your business and providing exceptional service. Leverage these resources, including training materials, marketing assets, and user guides.
  • CRM integration: Tanda can be integrated with your existing CRM system, allowing you to manage customer information and transactions efficiently.
  • Access to support channels: Tanda offers multiple support channels (email, phone, or in-app chat) to help agents address any questions or concerns.

D. Commission and Incentives

  • Commission structure: Tanda agents earn competitive commissions on customer transactions, such as withdrawals. The commission is based on the bank agent commission sharing structure and is shared between Tanda and the agent.
  • Bonus and reward programs: Tanda offers additional bonus and reward programs to incentivize agents for achieving specific performance milestones or participating in promotional activities.
  • Performance tracking: Monitor your performance regularly using Tanda's reporting and analytics tools.

Page 5: Merchant Onboarding - Account Setup

A. Account Creation and Verification

  1. Sign-up Process: To start using Tanda as a merchant, download the Tanda Trader App from your device's app store or dial *712# from your phone. Complete the registration form by providing accurate and up-to-date information about your business.
  2. Business Verification: Tanda will verify the legitimacy of your business during the registration process. This step ensures that only authorized businesses can use the platform.
  3. Required Documentation (KYC): Provide Know Your Customer (KYC) documentation. From the Merchant Training, this includes:
    • Business permit
    • National ID
    • Passport photo or selfie
    The Customer Onboarding Journey also mentions proof of address and tax identification number. Submit clear and legible copies.

Visual Chart: Merchant Sign-up Process

  1. Download Tanda Trader App / Dial *712#. (Alternatively, FAQ suggests online sign-up at org.tanda.africa first for some cases).
  2. Fill out the registration form with business details.
  3. Undergo business verification by TandaPay.
  4. Submit required KYC documents (Business Permit, National ID, Passport Photo/Selfie, potentially Proof of Address & Tax ID).
  5. Await account approval.

Page 6: Merchant Onboarding - Understanding Your Contract

What's a Contract?

A contract is a legally binding agreement that outlines the terms, conditions, and responsibilities between two or more parties. For TandaPay, Merchants must sign a service agreement to access the platform. This agreement defines the services provided, the merchant's obligations (such as fees and compliance with KYC laws), and conditions for termination. This contract ensures that both TandaPay and its merchants understand their responsibilities, comply with legal requirements, and build a trusted business relationship.

Signing the Merchant Contract

Based on the "TandaPay Merchant Training" document, merchants need to fill specific parts of the TANDA MERCHANT CONTRACT. Ensure all information is entered in the dotted-box spaces provided.

Visual Chart: Key Contract Signing Areas (Example)

Please refer to your specific Tanda Merchant Contract. Generally, you will need to provide information and signatures on:

  1. Page 1 (or near the beginning):
    • Effective Date (e.g., "1st day August 2024").
    • Merchant's Full Name / Business Name and P.O. BOX details where indicated for "the Merchant".
  2. Page 8 / Signature Page (or near the end):
    • SIGNED for and on behalf of [insert MERCHANT name]:
      • Your Name (e.g., "John Nyale").
      • Your Designation (e.g., "Shop owner").
      • Date.
      • Your Signature.
      • Seal/Stamp (if applicable).
    • WITNESSED BY for and on behalf of [insert Merchant name]: (This part seems to be for a witness for the merchant, ensure clarity on who signs here).
      • Witness Name (e.g., "Peter mwamgo").
      • Date.
      • Witness Signature.
    • Note: The example also showed "SIGNED for and on behalf of [insert BENEFICIARY name]". Clarify if this section applies to your specific merchant agreement type. If there's a separate beneficiary, their details (Name, Designation, Date, Signature) would go here.
